The Co-op Creamery Neighborhood Café is located in “the old creamery building,” a historically important landmark for co-ops, local food, and the food networks they create.

Led by chef Name Name, the building was originally home to the highly successful Franklin Co-op Creamery Association, a milk and delivery co-op that would come to produce 80 percent of the Twin Cities’ milk supply in its heyday.

The co-op that dominated the local food scene began as a group with just 13 members. They belonged to the Milk Wagon Drivers' Union, Local 471 organized on behalf of local dairy workers who worked seven days a week for unsustainably low wages. Believing in the power of co-ops, those 13 members incorporated themselves as “The Franklin Co-operative Creamery Association” in 1921, naming their co-operative venture after the avenue where their new plant was located.

Read more about the history of the Franklin Creamery in “A Remedy Invented by Labor,” by Steven Keillor.
